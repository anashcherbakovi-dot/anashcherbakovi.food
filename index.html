Khinkali

Khinkali is one of my favorite Georgian dishes. These are large dumplings traditionally filled with a mixture of minced meat, usually beef and pork, combined with onions, garlic, herbs, and spices. What makes khinkali special is the flavorful broth inside the dumpling that forms during cooking. They are boiled and served hot, and the traditional way to eat them is by holding the top knot, taking a bite, and carefully drinking the delicious broth before finishing the rest.

Main ingredients: dough, minced beef or pork, onions, garlic, black pepper, herbs, and broth.

Carbonara

Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish that I absolutely love. It is usually made with spaghetti, eggs, Pecorino Romano or Parmesan cheese, guanciale or pancetta, and freshly ground black pepper. The creamy texture of carbonara does not come from cream but from the combination of eggs and cheese mixed with the hot pasta, creating a rich and smooth sauce that coats every strand.

Main ingredients: spaghetti, eggs, Pecorino Romano or Parmesan cheese, guanciale or pancetta, and black pepper.

Satsivi

Satsivi is a traditional Georgian dish that is both unique and comforting. It is typically made with chicken served in a thick and flavorful walnut sauce. The sauce is prepared from ground walnuts, garlic, onions, and spices such as coriander, fenugreek, and sometimes a touch of vinegar. Satsivi is often served cold and has a rich, nutty flavor that makes it very distinctive in Georgian cuisine.

Main ingredients: chicken, ground walnuts, garlic, onions, coriander, fenugreek, and spices.
